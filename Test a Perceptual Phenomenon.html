Null hypothesis
H0 : tcongruent - tincongruent = 0
The time taken by average population for congruent and incongruent are equal.
Alternative hypothesis
H1 : tcongruent - tincongruent != 0
The time taken by average population is not equal for congruent and incongruent.

We will use dependent sample t-test (two tailed) beacuse :
This statistical procedure is used to determine whether the mean difference between two sets of observation is zero.
We have less than 30 samples.
We are assuming data is normally distributed.
Because of our null and alternative hypothesis we are using two tailed.

# Perform the statistical test here
# https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/tutorial/stats.html
from scipy import stats
#print(df.shape)
degreesOfFreedom = 23
stats.t.ppf(0.95, degreesOfFreedom)

# Calculates the T-test on TWO RELATED samples of scores, a and b.
stats.ttest_rel(df['Congruent'], df['Incongruent'])
Out[11]:
Ttest_relResult(statistic=-8.020706944109957, pvalue=4.1030005857111781e-08)

p value is under 0.05 therefore, we reject our null hypothesis. Thus, we can conclude that incongruent and congruent has different time taken by average population complete tasks.
